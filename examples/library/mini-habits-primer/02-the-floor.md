# 02 · The floor, not the ceiling

The number you set for `dailyTarget` is the **floor**, not a goal.

If you set it as a goal, you'll feel bad on the days you only hit the goal. ("I should have done more.")

If you set it as a floor, you'll feel proud on the days you only hit the floor. ("Even on a bad day, I cleared it.") And on most days, you'll do more anyway — because momentum is a real thing, but it needs a starting cost of approximately zero.

## A worked example

Suppose you want to build a writing habit.

| Approach | Target | What happens on a bad day |
|---|---|---|
| The motivated version | 1,000 words/day | "Too tired. Skip. Resets streak." |
| The Mini Habits version | 1 sentence/day | "Fine, one sentence." → often becomes 200 |

The motivated version optimises for the *good* day. The Mini Habits version optimises for the *bad* day. Bad days are more common than good ones, so optimising for them is the higher-leverage move.

## Calibrating the floor

Rule of thumb: pick a target so small that you'd feel silly saying "I didn't have time." Then halve it.

If your floor ever starts feeling hard, halve it again. The floor isn't supposed to be a workout — it's supposed to be a *signature*.
