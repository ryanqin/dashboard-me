# 03 · Streaks aren't the point

Streak counters are a UI affordance, not a goal. They exist to give your brain a tiny dopamine hit when you cross the floor — that's it.

Some honest things about streaks:

- **Long streaks are fragile.** A 90-day streak feels like an asset until you break it, and then the loss feels disproportionate. The longer the streak, the more terrified you get of losing it. That fear becomes its own kind of motivation, but it's a brittle one.
- **A streak is a leading indicator, not the thing itself.** The thing itself is "did the work happen". If you build for the streak instead of the work, you'll find yourself logging fake one-pushup days at 11:58pm just to keep the number alive. Don't.
- **Restarting is a feature, not a bug.** The number going from 47 → 0 → 1 isn't a failure. It's the system telling you what's actually true. The emotional cost of the reset is the price you pay for an honest signal.

## The dashboard's stance

This dashboard shows the streak. It also shows:

- The **daily completion ratio** (today's hits / today's trackers) — so you don't fixate on a single tracker.
- The **best streak ever** — so the current streak isn't the only number that matters.
- The **30-day heatmap** — so a missed day looks like one cell in a wall, not the end of the world.

The streak is one number among several. Treat it that way.

> The streak measures consistency. Consistency measures the system. The system is the actual thing.
