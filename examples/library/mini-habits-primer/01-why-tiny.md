# 01 · Why tiny

Most habit apps optimise for **streak shame**. You miss a day, the counter resets to zero, and your brain quietly files the whole thing as a loss.

Mini Habits flips that. The daily target is set so absurdly low that *showing up* always wins:

- One push-up.
- One page.
- One problem.
- One minute.

The point isn't the one. The point is that there is no "off day". You either did the trivial minimum, or you didn't — and the minimum is set so low that "I didn't" almost always means "I forgot," not "I couldn't."

## Why this works

Willpower is finite, motivation is fickle, but **identity compounds**. Every day you do the tiny thing, you're casting a small vote for the kind of person you want to be. You're not "trying to read more" — you're someone who reads, every day, even if just one page.

A streak of five hundred 1-minute days is a different beast than a streak of zero 30-minute days.

## How dashboard-me uses it

Every tracker has a `dailyTarget` — set it low. Like, embarrassingly low.

The streak counter doesn't care whether you blew past the target. It cares whether you crossed the floor. That's the whole UI philosophy in one sentence.

> Make the floor low enough that you can step over it on your worst day.

That's it. That's the book.
